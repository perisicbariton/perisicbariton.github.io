About Milan

Born in Novi Sad, Serbia, his music studies span across various fields of study: Accordion, Music theory, Composition (Bachelor's degree at the Academy of Arts in his hometown) and Voice (Master's degree at the Tartini Conservatory in Trieste). He was among the participants of the second edition of YAP Crescendo at the Teatro Real in Madrid.

In 2023 he won three consecutive first prizes at international competitions: Les Corts (Barcelona), Josep Palet (Martorell) and BA Lirica (Busto Arsizio) - in all of which he also won special prizes. 2023 has also seen him win the second prize at 40th Ciudad de Logroño, la Rioja international competition. In September he sang at the finals of Paris Opera Competition held at the legendary Théâtre des Champs Elysées.

Some newer acknowledgments come from the Sumi Jo International Singing Competition, where he was one of 11 finalists in the first edition held in July 2024 with over 500 applicants; the 20th Certamen de Nuevas Voces Ciudad de Sevilla, where he won second prize in November; and, the crown jewel of the year 2024, the VII Concurso Internacional de Canto "Compostela Lírica", where he won first prize.

Year 2025 has seen him winning the Festival de Música de Mafra "Filipe de Sousa" special prize at the 2nd Cascais Opera International Competition. This January he was one of the 16 finalists and a special prize winner at the 63rd Tenor Viñas Competition among record breaking 712 competitors.

Repertoire & Stage Experience

In his repertoire are roles such as Il Conte d'Almaviva (Le nozze di Figaro), Dottore Malatesta (Don Pasquale), Lescaut (Manon Lescaut), Marcello (La bohème), Don Giovanni, Oreste (Iphigénie en Tauride), Baritone (Carmina Burana), Harašta (The Cunning Little Vixen), Le Chat and L'horloge comtoise (L'enfant et les sortilèges).

In 2022 he created the lead role of Alonso in the world premiere of the Zarzuela El orgullo de quererte by Javier Carmena at the Teatros del Canal in Madrid. During the same year he debuted on the stage of Madrid's Teatro Real in a semi-staged concert with the JORCAM under the baton of Rubén Gimeno Martinez.

His concert activity includes varied music forms, such as song cycles by Britten, Fauré, Brahms, Vaughan Williams, De Falla, but also integral Zarzuela. He sang under the guidance of world's renowned conductors such as Simone Young, Emmanuelle Haïm, Giacomo Sagripanti, Shi Yeon Sung, Susanna Mälkki, Henrik Nánási, Jordi Savall, Luca Guglielmi, Mark Wigglesworth, John Adams, Josep Pons…

Among his next role debuts we find Schaunard (La Bohème), Cecil (Maria Stuarda), Heerrufer (Lohengrin).
